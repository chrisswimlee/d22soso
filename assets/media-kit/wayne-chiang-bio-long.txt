Wayne "D22-soso" Chiang — Long bio (for features, podcasts, and press)
=======================================================================

Wayne "D22-soso" Chiang helped define competitive gaming before it had a
mainstream name. As SoSOWAC he topped Case’s Ladder in Command & Conquer
(1996) and Warcraft II (1997). When StarCraft launched in 1998 he again hit
#1 in 1v1 and teams, won the first StarCraft tournament at Neutral Ground
Mountain View, and became StarCraft’s first play-by-play caster at PGL
Season 3.

On April 28, 1999, playing Random at Blizzard’s official Battle.net Season 1
Ladder Tournament, Chiang became the first Season 1 Brood War World Champion.
In a legendary Lost Temple semi-final against Guillaume “Grrrr…” Patry, he
rolled Protoss and broke a map largely controlled by Zerg with a three-pronged
assault. He later built early esports media infrastructure through GX StarCraft
and Pimpest Plays after Thresh and Calbear handed him the keys.

Beyond RTS, Chiang competed on the Magic: The Gathering Pro Tour (New York
2001, Boston 2002) and is publicly credited by Brian Weissman and Tom Martell
for originating curated draft pools that became Cube Draft. In Hearthstone he
reached Legend #35 Standard NA and #11 Wild NA.

Today he is Talent Manager for the World Series of Poker, overseeing the
official Vlogger Program, and inventor of patented casino games including
2 Hand Hold’em™ and Badugi Chase™. With Fortune 500 executive and WSOP
champion Joe Matton he co-authored Betting on Yourself: Winning in Business,
Poker, and the Age of AI (Kendall Hunt, December 2025).
